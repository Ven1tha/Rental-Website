A copyright is a type of intellectual property that gives its owner the exclusive right to copy and distribute a creative work, usually for a limited time. The creative work may be in a literary, artistic, educational, or musical form. Copyright is a tool to recognise your rights over your work. It doesn't prevent someone else from independently producing the same work. You can take legal action against any person who infringes your copyright by, for example, copying, selling, performing, or communicating your work without permission in New Zealand or in most countries overseas.
